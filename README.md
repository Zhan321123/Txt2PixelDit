# Txt2PixelDiT

2025/3/25

首先向各位模组开发者表示诚挚的歉意

本项目原先是一个文生像素图的AI模型，但由于训练集在没有征求同意的情况下使用了大量模组开发者们做制作的贴图，感到十分的抱歉，并对原先README里的“晚...”等不当言论深刻反思。

目前已经向大部分能够联系到的制作者们发送了道歉信，但是由于发送信封太多可能已经被标记为“垃圾邮件”，请注意查看邮件垃圾桶里是否有邮件；部分制作者仅找到了Bilibili账号所以只能B站私信，由于B站私信可能有屏蔽词并且仅能向陌生人发送一条消息，制作者们如果收到请回复以确保信息传递到位；目前仍有部分制作者未能找到有效的联系方式。

事情的经过大概是这样的：
最初，我在2024年制作我的第一款mod，仅仅制作了一堆没有实际功能的方块和物品，并且本人画的贴图也不好看，刚好当时AI也开始流行，于是我使用大模型生成图片，可是这些大模型生成的是高清图，仍无法绘制贴图。
后来我开始自己学习AI模型的构建，尝试各种方法生成贴图，但效果一直很差，输出贴图是纯黑或者随机颜色，并且也一直没有其他人关注我所做的东西（包括我的其他项目）。
直到最后一次，在2026年3月上旬的时候，我已经学习完了Diffusion、Transformer、Token等AI内容，决定开始再次构建这个项目，而且当时也玩过很多MC整合包，见识到了很多贴图材质非常优秀的mod，于是我就把这些mod的贴图解压出来，作为项目的训练集，并在README写下了“晚...”言论，因为我仍然认为没有人会关注的项目。
没有想到这次训练出来的效果远比之前做出来的好，尽管相比好看的贴图还差很多，但我还是兴奋地将模型分享到了qq群，当时大概是下午2~3点。
分享完之后，有人说我项目git提交不规范，大文件不应当放在github，而应该托管保存，随后他转发到其他群并说到：“已经开始吃瓜了”。
我当时以为他所说的“吃瓜”是我项目git不规范所以我并没有太在意。直到下午5点半，突然有另外的群友告诉我我摊上了大事，说已经有人要起诉我的侵权行为，当时我还不太相信，我一个普通人哪里用得着这么真实。直到他又转发给我了一些git issue(这也是我第一次收到issue)。
然后事情开始发酵，直到今天这个情况。

事情发展到今天我的问题确实很大，不应该在README里写下了不当的言论、不应当在没有征求同意的情况下私自使用别人的贴图、不应该传播侵权的内容、不应该不在第一时间删除项目、没有第一时间发布声明，学习开发却没有学习一点许可证... 我为此感到深刻的抱歉。

如果您能提供一些mod开发者的联系方式（指制作者github主页里没有提供邮箱的），我将不胜感激。

2025/3/25

First of all, I would like to express my sincere apologies to all module developers

This project was originally an AI model for generating pixel art. However, due to the use of a large number of textures created by mod developers in the training set without seeking their consent, I feel deeply sorry and have reflected deeply on the inappropriate remarks such as "late..." in the original README.

Apology letters have been sent to most of the creators who could be reached. However, due to the large number of envelopes sent, they may have been marked as "junk mail". Please check your spam folder for any emails. Currently, there are still some creators who have not been able to find effective contact information.

The whole story goes like this:
Initially, in 2024, I created my first mod, which merely consisted of a bunch of blocks and items without any practical functions. The textures I drew were also not very good. At that time, AI was also becoming popular, so I used large models to generate images. However, these large models generated high-definition images, which still couldn't be used for texture drawing.
Later, I started to learn building AI models on my own and tried various methods to generate textures, but the results were consistently poor. The output textures were either pure black or random colors, and no one else paid attention to what I was doing (including my other projects).
Until the last time, in early March 2026, I had finished learning AI content such as Diffusion, Transformer, and Token, and decided to start building this project again. At that time, I had also played many Minecraft (MC) mod packs and encountered many mods with excellent texture materials. So I extracted the textures from these mods and used them as the training set for the project, and wrote the comment "late..." in the README, because I still believed that no one would pay attention to this project.
I never expected the training results to be so much better than what I had previously achieved. Although the texture was far from perfect, I was still excited to share the model in the QQ group. It was around 2 or 3 PM at that time.
After sharing, someone pointed out that my project's Git commits were not standardized, and that large files should not be stored on GitHub but rather hosted elsewhere. He then forwarded the message to another group and commented, "I'm already enjoying the gossip.".
I initially thought that his comment about "eating melon" (a Chinese internet slang term referring to being an onlooker or spectator) was just a joke due to the irregularities in my project's Git repository, so I didn't pay much attention to it. It wasn't until 5:30 pm that another group member suddenly informed me that I was in serious trouble, saying that someone had filed a lawsuit against me for infringement. At first, I couldn't quite believe it; how could an ordinary person like me be involved in such a serious matter? It wasn't until he forwarded me some Git issues (which was also the first time I had ever received an issue) that I began to take it seriously.
Then things started to escalate, leading to the current situation today.

As things have progressed to this point, I acknowledge that my actions have been problematic. I should not have made inappropriate remarks in the README, used others' images without permission, disseminated infringing content, failed to promptly remove the project, or not issued a statement immediately. Furthermore, despite learning development, I have neglected to understand any licenses... I deeply apologize for these actions.

If you could provide me with the contact information of some mod developers (those whose email addresses are not provided on their creators' GitHub homepages), I would be extremely grateful.
